# Graph Analyses

## Q1 — Job Count by State (Top 20)
- **Texas and California dominate** the job posting volume, clearly standing above the rest of the states.
- After the top two, there is a **large drop-off** to the next tier (e.g., Florida, Virginia, Illinois, New York), suggesting job postings are concentrated in a few large labor markets.
- The remaining states form a **long tail** with gradually decreasing counts, indicating broader nationwide demand but at lower volumes per state.

## Q2 — Job Count by State (Retail Trade, Top 20)
- Retail Trade postings are **heavily concentrated in Texas**, with California a distant second.
- The next group (e.g., Florida, Arizona, Washington, Illinois, New York) shows **moderate activity**, but far below Texas.
- This pattern suggests Retail Trade demand is **not evenly distributed** and may reflect a combination of population size, retail footprint, and hiring intensity by region.

## Q3 — Percent Change in Job Postings (May 2024 → Sept 2024) for Selected Companies
- **Accenture shows the strongest growth**, increasing by roughly **~40%** from May to September 2024.
- **PricewaterhouseCoopers also grows**, by approximately **~35%**, indicating expanded hiring activity over the same period.
- **Deloitte declines sharply** (around **~-45%**), suggesting a significant slowdown or reduced job posting activity between those months.
- Overall, hiring trends can vary widely **even within similar peer companies**, showing that firm-specific factors matter.

## Q4 — Average Salary by Industry (Top 15)
- The **Information** industry has the **highest average salary midpoint** (around the low **$80k** range), leading the chart.
- **Finance and Insurance** and **Public Administration** appear next (around the low **$60k** range), forming a high-paying cluster.
- Mid-tier industries include categories like **Utilities, Construction, Professional/Scientific/Technical Services**, and **Retail Trade** (roughly mid **$40k–$60k**).
- The spread across industries indicates **meaningful wage differences** by sector, likely tied to skill requirements and role composition.

## Q5 — Top 10 Most Frequently Mentioned Skills
- **Communication** is the most frequently mentioned skill, emphasizing the importance of soft skills across job roles.
- **Data Analysis** ranks highly, reflecting strong demand for analytical capability across many industries.
- **Management, Leadership, Problem Solving, Operations, and Project Management** appear prominently, suggesting many roles require coordination, decision-making, and execution skills.
- Technical/role-specific skills like **SQL (Programming Language)** also appear in the top 10, showing that **data-oriented tools** remain a common requirement.
